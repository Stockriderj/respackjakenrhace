Origin

Gene Transfer

Respackjakenrhace does not live on this world. Well, not the mainland, anyway. See, the Respak origin story extends far beyond the Jenkaen Tribe, or even the Uytemuijil Tribe that came before it. Its people arrived to Pacifica long ago, while it was still inhabited by primitive Pacificans. Respakia, the Rhacenese's origin planet on, was ejected from its parent star system after it came too close to the Pacifican Solar System. It soon obtained an orbit around the Sun which crisscrossed through the orbits of Parva, Balteuma, Proxima...and Pacifica, which is the secondary protagonist in this interplanetary play.

Now you're probably wondering, "How did the ancient Rhacenese get from Respakia to Pacifica?" Good question. It's an interesting story - and, as always, it starts with an asteroid. The asteroid, which Respak scientists now dub the Respak-Pacifica Boat, found its way to Respakia one day. It cut through the atmosphere at such a speed that it scraped off a chunk of the earth there, and it then exited the atmosphere of Respakia.

As it turned out, the chunk of land it scraped off contained billions of Respak genes. These genes were then protected by the rock that had covered it; due to the friction from the impact, it had been morphed and fused itself over the air pocket with the genes in it. Coincidence? I think not.

Respak-Pacifica Boat then found its way to Pacifica.

The tiny asteroid hit the planet and the genes in the pocket burst out, infecting the population that lived there. However, Respak genes, you see, aren't the same as human genes. Although the Respak are extremely similar to humans nowadays due to all the evolution that occurred, their genes are built different. They can transfer memories. They can also broadcast thoughts to other Respaks if desired. So the Respak didn't even have to talk until meeting new species. They used this to communicate with their home planet Respakia as it spiraled into the orbit of Visi and became a natural satellite. This brings us into stage two of Respak history: evolution.

Two-World Evolution

So the Respak now live on two planets - great! Only, they don't. The genes had to first infect the native population. After this came millions of years of evolution that blended the Respak and human species together.
